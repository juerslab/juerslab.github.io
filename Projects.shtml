Crystallography, Biophysics and Protein Structure


Projects. Our work can be thought of in two broad categories:

1. The physical chemistry of macromolecular crystals.
  • Dehydration effects during crystal mounting
  • Physical behavior of macromolecular crystals related to their nanoporous architecture 
  • Intermolecular interactions for protein crystallization
2.  Structural Biology - Using X-ray diffraction to study macromolecules of interest
  • PcpA - an enzyme with potential for applications in bioremediation - in collaboration with Prof. Tim Machonkin
  • MntR - manganese transport regulator - in collaboration with Prof. Arthur Glasfeld, Reed College
  • E. coli beta-galactosidase
  • Other projects: We've also worked on the following molecules, all of which have resisted crystallization:
    • Myelin Basic Protein - an autoantigen involved in multiple sclerosis and an intrinsically unstructured protein
    • PIRL - genes important for pollen development (in collaboration with Prof. Dan Vernon).
    • Human ALDH1 - an enzyme involved in retinoic acid synthesis (in collaboration with Prof. James Russo)
